Elevator pitch

I am a fourth-year Computer Science student at UVA interested in building practical software and understanding the systems behind it. My eP shows projects across software engineering, databases, cybersecurity, networks, AI, and self-directed practice.

The main argument is simple: I do not want to be judged only by labels like course numbers or tools. I want the reader to see what I built, what problem it responded to, and what I learned from the process.

1

What changed

I moved from organizing by class chronology to organizing by reader-friendly project names and clearer evidence.

2

What worked

The strongest pages connect personal narrative to artifacts. They show a pattern instead of asking the reader to invent one.

3

What comes next

The site can keep growing after the course as projects become more polished and new evidence replaces older examples.
